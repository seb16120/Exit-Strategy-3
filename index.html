    HOW TO PLAY

    Rules

    Win

    Win by getting two of your five numbered pawns out through the central exit, or by having your Hunter capture three opposing pawns.

    Numbered pawns

    A pawn moves horizontally or vertically as far as possible. It stops on the last empty square before a wall or any piece. Pawns never capture.

    The exit is intangible: crossing it does nothing. A pawn exits only when the central exit is its forced final square.

    Hunter

    The Hunter moves exactly one square horizontally or vertically. It cannot enter the exit, land on a friendly pawn, or land on the opposing Hunter. It captures by entering a square occupied by an opposing pawn.

    Setup and turns

    The choice maker is drawn at random and chooses to play first or second. The first player becomes Player 1 (cyan), sets up first, and takes the first turn. Both setups remain secret until the simultaneous reveal.

    CPU1

    CPU1 uses a random legal setup and checks the opponent's immediately available replies before moving.

    CPU3

    CPU3 uses a scored logical setup and searches three plies: CPU move, opponent reply, CPU reply. It thinks for at least one second and no longer than 45 seconds.

    CPU+

    CPU+ deepens its minimax search for at least 30 seconds and at most 55 seconds. It may move after one second when it has only one legal move or a move wins the game immediately. Its setup receives five extra seconds of analysis and uses local, weighted placement results.

    CPU vs CPU

    Select CPU1, CPU3 or CPU+ independently for Player 1 and Player 2. The match can be paused, resumed, or advanced one move at a time.

    Timed game

    When enabled, each move has a one-minute limit and each player has 50 minutes total. The confirmation dialog does not pause the clock. A forced pass immediately consumes one full minute from that player's total.

    Abandoning

    “I was going to lose” awards the opponent a win and may be used by CPU+ learning. “I have to leave” ends the game without recording a result.

    Draws

    The game is drawn after two consecutive forced passes, the third occurrence of the same position, or 100 turns total (50 per player).
